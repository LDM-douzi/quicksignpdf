QuickSignPDF

Privacy Policy

QuickSignPDF is intended to let people fill, sign, annotate, and export PDFs directly in the browser. The current version is designed to avoid server-side document processing by default. This page explains what personal data may still be processed when someone visits or uses the site.

Operator: Loïc De Mulder

Contact email: loicdm@outlook.com

Last updated: 17 April 2026

Who this policy applies to

This policy applies to visitors to the QuickSignPDF website and users of the browser-based editor. It is intended for a free hobby project operated from Belgium and directed at users in the European Union and elsewhere.

How the editor works

The editor runs client-side in the browser. The app does not require an account. In the current version, PDFs that users open in the editor are meant to remain on their own device unless the user chooses to export or share a file themselves.

Personal data the operator does not intentionally request

QuickSignPDF does not intentionally require users to submit accounts, names, payment details, or uploaded documents to the operator in order to use the core editor features. The app is designed so that PDF editing happens locally in the browser.

Personal data that may still be processed

Even though the editor is designed to work locally, some personal data may still be processed through normal website operation. This can include:

  • Technical request data such as IP address, browser information, timestamps, and network logs collected by the hosting or delivery provider.
  • Locally stored browser data if the user enables autosave, such as the current PDF session, annotations, and editor preferences.
  • Any personal data a user includes inside a PDF, typed text, signature, note, or image stored locally in their own browser.

Browser storage and autosave

QuickSignPDF may store data in the user’s browser, including IndexedDB or local storage, to preserve editor preferences and optionally restore an unfinished session after refresh. Autosave can be turned off in the editor. If autosave is disabled, the saved local session is cleared by the app. Clearing browser storage can also remove saved sessions.

Cookies and similar technologies

QuickSignPDF does not intentionally use advertising cookies, tracking cookies, or user profiling technologies in the current version. The app may use browser storage that is functionally necessary to remember the autosave preference or a local editing session. Hosting providers or infrastructure services may still use strictly necessary technical mechanisms for security, caching, or delivery.

Purposes and legal bases

To the extent personal data is processed by or for the operator, the main purposes are:

  • making the website available to visitors;
  • protecting the site against abuse, misuse, and technical attacks;
  • remembering a user’s local editor preference or locally saved session when that feature is enabled.

The relevant legal bases may include the operator’s legitimate interests in providing and securing the site, and where applicable, the user’s request to use a functional storage feature such as autosave.

Third-party hosting and infrastructure

If the site is hosted through providers such as Vercel, Netlify, Cloudflare, or similar infrastructure providers, those providers may process technical request data under their own policies and retention practices. Users should also review the applicable privacy information of those providers where relevant.

Data sharing

The operator does not intend to sell personal data or knowingly share user PDF contents for advertising. Technical data may nevertheless be processed by hosting, CDN, DNS, or other infrastructure providers involved in serving the site.

Retention

Local session data remains in the user’s browser until it is cleared by the app, removed by the user, or deleted by the browser. Technical server or infrastructure logs, if any, are retained according to the relevant provider’s configuration and policies.

International transfers

If the hosting or infrastructure provider uses systems outside Belgium or the European Economic Area, personal data such as technical request logs may be processed outside the EEA subject to the provider’s own safeguards and legal mechanisms.

Your rights

To the extent the operator processes personal data as a controller, users in the EU may have rights under applicable data protection law, including rights of access, rectification, erasure, restriction, objection, and complaint to a supervisory authority. In Belgium, the supervisory authority is the Belgian Data Protection Authority.

Children

QuickSignPDF is not specifically directed to children. Users should not rely on the site to store sensitive information for minors or any critical records without their own backup and legal review.

Changes to this policy

This policy may be updated from time to time to reflect changes to the website, hosting setup, or legal obligations. The version published on this page is the version that applies at the time of use.
